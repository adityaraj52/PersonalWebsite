VOTERS

Voting Outliers Based-on Randomised Sampling

Abstract

Finding patterns in real data is troublesome as the normality condition does not hold for most of the measured dimensions. Existing algorithms generally fail to detect multi-variate outliers in non-normally distributed data. This research is towards the problem of solving multi-variate outlier detection problem in high dimensional space for non-uniform distribution of data set along the mean. It is based on the idea of modifying location and scatter estimates in Mahalanobis distance followed by Mahalnobis-chisquare measure votes at adapted significance level based on the heterogeneity of the data set. The mathematical foundation of the research work comes from areas such as dimensionality reduction using Principal component analysis, bootstrapping techniques to randomise sampling in the cluster to generate votes for each data and finally statistical confidence analysis of chi-square Mahalanobis data plot based on heterogeneity level of the data set.
